Who we are when we are one

We celebrate what is great, we know each other as people, and we have each other's back. We name what is not working, we bring the fix, and once a decision is made, we stand behind it together.

We know each other as people.
We are more than our titles and our rooms. We make the time to know the person behind the work, not just the work.
We shine a light on the wins.
We catch each other doing it right and we say so, out loud and often. Naming what is working is as much our job as naming what is not.
We keep what happens in our team, in our team.
What is said in the room stays in the room. That is what makes it safe to be honest with each other.
We lead our rooms out loud.
When something in my room is not working, I say it first, early, and plainly. When it sits in yours, I come to you to help, not around you to talk about it.
We bring the solution with the problem.
If it is mine to carry, I show up with a first step, not just the weight of it. Venting names the problem. It does not move it.
We offer help before anyone asks.
I do not WAIT to be asked. I notice where you are stretched and I step in. I put my hand up in the hallway, and I say yes when yours is up.
We say the actual thing, and we say it with care.
Frank and kind are not opposites. If I have feedback, I give you the real version in plain words. No hinting, no talking around it, no "my way or nothing." I take any personnel issue, concern, or important update straight to the person who needs it, never around them. The only test is what is best for our kids and for this team.
We disagree in the room, then we rally.
I bring my honest take while the call is still open. Once it is made, I carry it like I made it. No "I knew that would not work" in the hallway after.
We treat whole-school work as ours.
Every staff-facing and school-facing event, initiative, and decision gets my full weight, whether or not my name is on it.
★ That is all nine. We are the team, and this is our house.
The undercurrent: Excellence

Excellence is what keeps us honest about results. It means catching a problem while it is small, fixing it, and offering a hand before anyone has to ask. It also means EVERYONE is held accountable to the urgent pursuit of results. And it begins with each of us: we always use the mirror before the magnifying glass. As Maya Angelou said, when you know better, do better.

What this makes possible for the full school
  • We become one, and staff feel the shift in adult culture before we ever name it.
  • You get the same answer from any one of us. That consistency is what makes people feel safe.
  • Because we are aligned, any of us can give any of us feedback, and it lands as care.
